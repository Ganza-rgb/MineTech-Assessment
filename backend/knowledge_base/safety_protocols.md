# MineTech Rwanda — Safety Protocols & Hazard Response

## 1. Underground Safety Protocols

### Ventilation & Air Quality
- **Continuous monitoring:** All active shafts equipped with multi-gas sensors (CH₄, CO, CO₂, O₂)
- **Alert thresholds:**
  - CH₄ > 1.0%: notify shift supervisor
  - CH₄ > 1.5%: initiate evacuation
  - CO > 35ppm: evacuate within 5 minutes
  - O₂ < 19.5%: evacuate immediately
- **Ventilation drops:** If airflow falls below 0.3 m/s for >2 minutes, trigger ERR-902 and evacuate immediate zone

### Structural Integrity
- **Daily inspections:** geologist + engineer walkthrough of active stopes
- **Micro-fracture monitoring:** strain gauges alert at 0.5mm displacement
- **ERR-104 (Structural Strain):** Restrict all heavy machinery within 50m radius instantly
- **Post-blast inspection:** minimum 30-minute wait + air quality check before re-entry

### Fire & Emergency Response
- **Fire extinguishers:** located every 50m in main drives, inspected monthly
- **Refuge chambers:** equipped with 36-hour air supply, communication radio, first aid
- **Emergency muster points:** marked with reflective signage, headcount protocol
- **SOS protocol:** press MT-TAG SOS button → surface control room alerted + GPS location broadcast

## 3. Equipment Safety

### Pre-Start Inspection Checklist
1. Fluid levels (oil, hydraulic, coolant)
2. Tire condition and pressure
3. Brake function test
4. Lighting and warning horns
5. Fire extinguisher presence
6. Seatbelt and ROPS (Roll-Over Protective Structure) integrity

### Immobilized Equipment Protocol
- Activate hazard beacons + reflective triangles
- Notify dispatch via Minetech OS
- If blocking haulage: winch to Zone C within 45 minutes
- Log incident in equipment maintenance module

## 4. Environmental Hazards

### Dust & Particulate Matter
- **Dust suppression:** water sprayers on drill rigs, conveyor covers
- **Respiratory protection:** P2 masks required in high-dust zones
- **Monitoring:** real-time PM2.5/PM10 sensors at strategic points

### Noise Exposure
- **Limit:** 85dB(A) for 8-hour TWA
- **Protection:** ear defenders mandatory above 85dB
- **Monitoring:** dosimetry badges rotated monthly

### Heat Stress
- **Wet-bulb globe temperature (WBGT) monitoring** in deep open pits
- **WBGT > 28°C:** mandatory rest breaks every 30 minutes
- **WBGT > 30°C:** suspend non-essential outdoor work

## 5. Incident Reporting

### Classification Levels
- **Level 1 (Near Miss):** no injury, potential for harm — report within 24h
- **Level 2 (First Aid):** minor injury requiring first aid — report within 8h
- **Level 3 (Medical Treatment):** injury requiring clinic/hospital — immediate report
- **Level 4 (Fatality / Major Incident):** immediate evacuation + emergency services

### Reporting Workflow
1. Scene secured + first aid administered
2. Supervisor notified via Minetech OS mobile app
3. Photographic evidence + witness statements uploaded
4. Safety officer investigates within 24h (Level 1–2) or immediately (Level 3–4)
5. Root-cause analysis + corrective actions logged in system
