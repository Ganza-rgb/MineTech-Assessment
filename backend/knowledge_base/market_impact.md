# MineTech Rwanda — Market Impact & Growth

## 1. Problem We Solve

80% of African mining operations still rely on **paper-based systems**, creating critical gaps in:
- **Worker safety:** delayed hazard response, incomplete incident records
- **Regulatory compliance:** missed reporting deadlines, incomplete documentation
- **Operational efficiency:** manual data entry errors, slow decision-making
- **Financial accountability:** untraceable production, revenue leakage

## 2. Market Opportunity

**African mining sector:**
- ~30% of world's mineral reserves
- 30% of global exploration activity
- Major gap in affordable digital solutions for mid-tier and artisanal operations

**Enterprise alternatives** (SAP, Oracle, Hexagon) cost 5–75× more than MineTech solutions, putting them out of reach for most African operations.

## 3. Growth Trajectory

### 2019
- Founded in Kigali, Rwanda by Kelvin Rwihimba
- Initial focus: digitizing artisanal cobalt and tin cooperatives in Rwanda

### 2021–2022
- Expanded to commercial-scale mines (tantalum, tungsten, tin)
- Deployed first IoT sensor networks in underground operations
- Achieved RMB regulatory compliance certification

### 2023
- **150+ mining cooperatives** on waitlist for sensor tech deployment
- Selected for **UNDP timbuktoo MineTech Accelerator Programme**
- Pan-African expansion initiated (DRC, Uganda, Tanzania)

### 2024–2025
- Full platform launch: Minetech OS + Minetech Trace
- Grade control AI model deployed at 3 commercial sites
- Achieved 80% reduction in compliance reporting time for clients

## 4. UNDP timbuktoo Accelerator

MineTech was selected for the prestigious **timbuktoo MineTech Accelerator Programme**, a major continental incubation initiative launched by the **United Nations Development Programme (UNDP)** to elevate youth-led tech disruptions across African supply chains.

### Programme Benefits
- Seed funding and investor access
- Technical mentorship from global mining experts
- Regulatory navigation support across African jurisdictions
- Network access to 50+ mining operators across 12 countries
